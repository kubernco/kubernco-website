Assumptions & Limitations

Every fixed assumption in this model runs against the upside. The prenatal-to-delivery rate is set at 50% — meaning the model already assumes half of all enrolled mothers deliver elsewhere. The revenue figure excludes NICU, postpartum, and GYN follow-up entirely. The decline rate uses the 20-year historical average, not the steeper recent pace. Despite these haircuts, the engagement produces a healthy LTV:CAC and positive ROI at default settings. Sliding any assumption toward reality only improves the numbers.

Fixed inputs. Retainer ($12,500/month), cost per click ($5, Memphis maternity search), organic enrollments (6/month), and delivery decline (−153/year, 20-year average — Q3→Q4 2025 data suggests this is conservative). LTV is 1.75× revenue per delivery: 1 guaranteed first delivery + 75% probability of return for a second (census-verified average of 2.0 children per mother). Retained mothers compound in the chart; the Year 1 KPI reflects first deliveries only.

The one operating assumption. The funnel runs — ads generate clicks at the modeled CPC, inquiries convert at the modeled rate, and enrollment follow-up closes them. If any stage underperforms, the levers above update every output in real time.

What this model doesn't count. Downstream revenue from NICU, postpartum, and GYN follow-up — omitted here, additive to LTV if included. The ACGME residency threshold: at −153/year, Regional One falls below 2,000 annual deliveries by mid-2027. That program review risk has no revenue expression.
