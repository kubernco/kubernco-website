# Kubern Co — Website Build Brief
## For Claude Code / Developer Handoff

---

## Project Overview

**Company:** Kubern Co LLC
**Owner:** Jeremy Graves
**Service:** AI-native healthcare marketing strategy, specializing in labor and delivery programs for hospitals
**Target Client:** Hospital marketing directors and L&D program administrators
**Goal:** A single-page scroll-reveal website that builds emotional resonance and ends in a contact form

---

## Design Direction

- **Style:** Parallax scroll-reveal. Content fades/slides in as the user scrolls. Each section feels like a new moment revealing itself.
- **Tone:** Human, warm, editorial. NOT clinical. NOT corporate. Think intimate documentary photography aesthetic.
- **Typography:** Distinctive serif display font for headers. Clean, readable body font. Avoid Inter, Roboto, Arial.
- **Color palette:** Soft, warm neutrals. Deep navy or slate for text. Avoid sterile whites and hospital blues.
- **Hero image:** A human, intimate moment—mother and newborn skin-to-skin, shot from behind or side. Or a pregnant woman in a quiet anticipatory moment. Natural light. No hospital equipment visible if possible.
- **Motion:** Scroll-triggered fade and slide-in animations per section. Background parallax on hero. Subtle, not distracting.
- **Layout:** Single page. Sections stack vertically. Mobile-first responsive.

## Technical Notes

- Single HTML file preferred, or React if scroll animations require it
- Scroll-reveal library: use AOS (Animate On Scroll) or GSAP ScrollTrigger
- Hero section: full viewport height, parallax background image
- Fonts: load from Google Fonts — suggest pairing like Playfair Display + Lora, or similar editorial serif combination
- No generic stock photo placeholder — use a warm, human image or a solid color treatment with a texture overlay until final photography is sourced
- Contact form: static form pointing to Formspree or similar, no backend required
- Mobile responsive: all sections should reflow cleanly on 375px viewport

---

## Brand Voice Notes

- Warm but not soft. Confident but not arrogant.
- Speak to the hospital marketing director, but always with the mother in frame.
- Avoid clinical language. Avoid jargon. Avoid generic healthcare marketing tone.
- Short sentences. Earned emotion. No fluff.
