Before You Read Further

What You Need to Know

On reversing the curve: Phase 1 target: +13 deliveries/month. The three-county market produces 1,238 deliveries/month. At a 50% prenatal-to-delivery conversion rate, +13 deliveries requires only 26 prenatal signups per month — a 2.1% capture rate from a market where no competitor is tracking and Regional One is currently invisible. The constraint is not the goal. It is that no data exists today to identify where those 26 women are being lost.

This is a playbook, not a proposal. The deliverables inside are intentionally exhaustive — every line item is a required condition for the step above it to succeed. Built on the frameworks of Alex Hormozi — one of the most documented direct-response marketers working today, with an average lifetime customer LTV of 36:1 from his approach — this funnel stack outlines everything required to make this system succeed and drive one outcome: more deliveries at Regional One Health.

Minimal oversight by design. This playbook is built to run with the marketer executing the full system. Leadership does not manage it. The goal is to keep your team focused on clinical operations while the marketing engine runs behind it — with clear reporting so you always know what's happening.

On branding. The L&D brand identity will remain consistent with Regional One Health while maintaining a distinct presence from the broader hospital. No significant change to the overall brand. OB/GYN maintains its own lane and affiliation — without disrupting the main hospital identity.

Counter-Narrative Content
Gate — Must Be Live Before Ad Spend As-Needed

Blocks ad spend. Every paid click sends someone to Google. Without counter-narrative content, the first result is the malpractice verdict.

  • Audit current search results for all brand terms
  • Identify top 5 active negative narratives — malpractice, "The Med" stigma, safety perception
  • Develop counter-narrative strategy for each — not defensive, identity-building
  • Write 3–5 SEO-optimized counter-narrative articles
  • Publish, index, and verify ranking before any paid traffic launches
  • Build CNM identity narrative — separate mental category from "The Med"
  • Ongoing monitoring — alert on new negative coverage
  • Response pieces as new narratives emerge

What Scaling Looks Like

Phase 2

Locked

Unlocks after 30 days of stable tracked funnel data and confirmed conversion rates at each stage.

Referral + Affiliate Program
Structured incentive system for OB referrals, doulas, and community health workers. Requires funnel tracking to measure attributed deliveries.
Growth Package
Scaling beyond +13/month. Requires additional resources — budget, staff, infrastructure. Assessed after Phase 1 baseline is proven.
Display + YouTube Ads
Brand-building layer. Runs on top of a profitable search and Meta foundation. Requires CNM video assets and larger ad budget.
Local Content Creator
Embedded recurring production after proof of concept is established. Scales content output without scaling time.
Patient Retention Newsletter
Past patient loyalty, repeat pregnancies, and word-of-mouth activation. Separate list and strategy from prospective patient nurture.
Podcast + Long-Form Content
Authority-building content — CNM interviews, birth expert conversations. Higher production value, compounding SEO and social equity.
